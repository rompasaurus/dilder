## What Is This Part?

An 8-pin flat cable connector that links the Dilder PCB to the Waveshare 2.13" e-paper display module. The display connects via a short flat flex cable (FFC) with a JST SH plug on the PCB end.

JST SH is 1.0mm pitch — half the pitch of the JST PH battery connector (2.0mm). The smaller pitch allows 8 signals in a compact footprint, which is important since the connector sits between the ESP32-S3 module and the board edge.

---

## The JST SH Series

JST SH (Subminiature Header) connectors are the standard for small sensor and display connections in modern electronics. They're the same connectors used by:

- **Adafruit STEMMA QT** — 4-pin JST SH for I2C sensors
- **SparkFun Qwiic** — also 4-pin JST SH for I2C
- **Waveshare displays** — various pin counts for display interfaces

The 1.0mm pitch is small enough for compact designs but large enough for hand-soldering if needed (with a fine tip and steady hands). The horizontal (right-angle) variant used here keeps the cable parallel to the board, reducing the vertical height of the assembly.

## The SPI Interface to the E-Paper Display

The e-paper display uses **SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface)** — a 4-wire synchronous serial bus:

- **SCK (Clock)** — clock signal generated by the ESP32 (master). Data is sampled on the rising edge.
- **MOSI (Master Out, Slave In)** — data from ESP32 to display. One bit per clock cycle, MSB first.
- **CS (Chip Select)** — pulled LOW to select this device. When HIGH, the display ignores all SPI traffic.
- **DC (Data/Command)** — additional signal specific to displays. LOW = command byte, HIGH = data byte.

Two additional control signals:
- **RST** — hardware reset. Pulse LOW for >10ms to reset the display controller.
- **BUSY** — output from the display. HIGH while the display is performing a refresh (which takes 2-3 seconds for a full update on e-ink). The ESP32 must wait for BUSY to go LOW before sending new data.

SPI is faster than I2C (up to 10+ MHz vs 400 kHz) and better suited for bulk data transfer. A full frame buffer for the 2.13" display (250 x 122 pixels, 1-bit) is about 3.8 KB — at 10 MHz SPI, this transfers in under 4 milliseconds.
